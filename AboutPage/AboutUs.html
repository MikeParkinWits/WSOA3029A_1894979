About The Website

Hello and welcome to lost data.! Now, you’re probably wondering what this website is all aout, so let me tell you.

Data has increasingly become the new currency of the 21st Century, specifically when it comes to our own personal data that we entrust with big tech companies – such as Facebook, LinkedIn, and many more. However, as we increasingly entrust more and more of this data with these companies, there are seemingly more and more data breaches that occur – resulting in our data being stolen, or ‘lost’ by the big companies. Thus, this website is about teaching the public the risk that comes with sharing your personal data with big companies – referring specifically to sharing information digitally, as that is where peoples most valuable data lies.

To do this, I will be utilizing the HaveIBeenPawned API – which provides multiple pieces of information on data breaches that have occurred – to create various data visualizations, using the D3.js library, in the hopes of bringing awareness to just how much data has been ‘lost’ by these big companies. While the use of data visualization might not be necessary, as writing an article highlighting this information could also be valuable, it serves as an extremely effective way of teaching people. Not only is data visualization an extremely useful way to get your point across, but when performed correctly it can have an everlasting effect on the viewer.

On top of this, I also aim to use this website to post blogs about importance of data visualization, highlighting elements that should be kept in mind when creating them. This done with the goal of both teaching the importance of data visualizations, and in the hopes of encouraging others to create good data visualizations to spread information about topics they’re passionate about.

So, I hope you stick around to learn a thing or two about data breaches, questioning just how much data you give up daily and how easily it can be ‘lost’. Not only that, but I also hope you read my blog to learn a few tips and tricks about creating effective data visualizations of your own!
