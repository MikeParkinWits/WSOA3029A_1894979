Initial Website Styleguide

10 September 2021 | Words: 448 | Website Development

In this blog post we will look at the style guide for my website, which can be seen above. Although some may see it as extremely bland and boring, illustrating that the design for my website will be extremely simple, it was chosen to illustrate my minimalistic preferences. However, this design was not just chosen because of my personal preferences but was also deliberate – which I will outline throughout this blog post.

To start, we can look at the colour palette chosen, which consists of various shades of greys. While I considered utilizing colour palettes consisting of brighter colours, such as reds and blues, I ultimately decided to stray away from these as the main purpose of my website is to showcase data visualizations. As such, I did not want to over stimulate the users with too much colour; instead, I chose for the focus to be purely on the data visualizations and their potential use of colour to highlight important information. However, this may change in future as the addition of an accent colour might be implemented to contrast the more neutral shades.

Next, we can look at the text size and font. As we can see from the style guide, all the headings utilize the Poppins font, which helps them stand out and be more readable. In comparison to this, Helvetica Light is utilized for all body fonts – as it allows for more words to fit in smaller spaces, while still being legible. Not only that, but each text element has also been sized appropriately, allowing for a good visual hierarchy of importance between elements.

Thereafter, we can look to the interactable elements – buttons and the links. Although minimalism is one of my websites core design inspirations, it was not done at the expense of usability. In fact, every interactable element on the site was carefully considered, with each changing in some way when hovered over. This can best be seen when looking at two elements: buttons, which invert their colours, increase in size slightly, and gain a shadow; and the navigation menu, which utilizes an animated line to indicate the link currently selected.

Lastly, we can look at the branding of the lost data website. While this is not as vital as the overall design of the website, it is still important to consider – as it should match the overall design language of the site. Here I present two logo variations, with the one on the left being the main logo utilized in the navigation bar, and the one on the right being a more simplified version to be used in places such as the website favicon – as it is more readable at smaller scales.
